Trigonometry Practice Problems

Solve the Unknown Side

Problem 1

Given a right-angled triangle with an angle of 67° and the hypotenuse equal to 14. Find the length of the side opposite to the 67° angle (labeled as x).

Hint: Use the sine function.

Problem 2

Given a triangle with angles 90°, 45°, and another angle. The hypotenuse is 20, and one of the legs is labeled as 'h' (the height). Find the value of 'h'.

Hint: Since it's a 45-45-90 triangle, the legs are equal. You can also use sine or cosine.

Problem 3

Given a right-angled triangle with a 30° angle and the adjacent side equal to 14 cm. Find the length of the side opposite to the 30° angle (labeled as x).

Hint: Use the tangent function.
